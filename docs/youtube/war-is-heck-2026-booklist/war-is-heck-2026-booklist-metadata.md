üìö **2026 Reading List:**

**January:** *The Power and the Glory* by Graham Greene
A whiskey priest on the run in anti-Catholic Mexico must choose between survival and faithfulness.

**February:** *Starship Troopers* by Robert Heinlein
Juan Rico learns what duty, citizenship, and sacrifice really mean through brutal training and alien warfare.

**March:** *The Fellowship of the Ring* by J.R.R. Tolkien
A hobbit inherits a ring of terrible power and sets out with eight companions to destroy it.

**April:** *Leviathan Wakes* by James S.A. Corey
A detective and ship captain uncover a conspiracy that could ignite war across the solar system.

**May:** *The Three Musketeers* by Alexandre Dumas
D'Artagnan finds brotherhood with three legendary swordsmen as they fight for honor, loyalty, and their king.

**June:** *The Mote in God's Eye* by Larry Niven & Jerry Pournelle
Humanity's first contact with aliens becomes a test of mercy, wisdom, and whether we can truly love the other.

**July:** *The Two Towers* by J.R.R. Tolkien
The fellowship scatters, Frodo and Sam journey deeper into enemy territory while their friends fight desperate battles.

**August:** *Master and Commander* by Patrick O'Brian
Captain Jack Aubrey navigates friendship and naval combat‚Äîuntil one disastrous choice costs him everything.

**September:** *The Screwtape Letters* by C.S. Lewis
A senior demon instructs his nephew on how to tempt a human soul to damnation. The real war is spiritual.

**October:** *Hyperion* by Dan Simmons
Seven pilgrims journey to meet the Shrike, each telling their story of suffering, love, and redemption.

**November:** *The Return of the King* by J.R.R. Tolkien
The final battle for Middle-Earth. Frodo completes his mission through failure and grace. Victory comes through sacrifice, not strength.
