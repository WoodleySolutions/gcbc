## CHANGES MADE:

### **1. Removed Repetitive "Duty. Joy. Self-mastery." Refrain**

**Location:** Line 202 (middle of Christ section)

**Before:**
> *"Duty. Joy. Self-mastery. In that moment, He practiced self-mastery..."*

**After:**
> *"In that moment, He practiced self-mastery..."*

**Why:** Refrain now appears only at beginning (Nelson section) and end (final recap) as bookends. Middle instance felt repetitive.

---

### **2. ADDED: Deep "WHY" Section on Duty (MAJOR ADDITION)**

**Location:** Lines 136-164 (Problem section, after "buying permission to be resentful")

**What was added:**
- New philosophical section explaining WHY having a good attitude is a duty
- Generational legacy argument: kids are watching to learn if family is worth wanting
- Vocation/calling language (without mentioning conversion details)
- Connection to Nelson (his men needed to see the mission was worth it)
- Positive framing: building a legacy for generations

**Key new lines:**
> *"Because my kids are watching me to learn whether having a family is worth wanting."*
>
> *"If I show up every day in a bad mood, resentful, like fatherhood is a burden—what am I teaching them? I'm telling them that having a family of their own is a dismal chore they should avoid."*
>
> *"But if I show up cheerful, eager, like there's nothing I'd rather be doing—I'm showing them that family is a good thing. Something worth aspiring to."*
>
> *"And that's how you build a legacy that lasts for generations."*
>
> *"That's why it's a duty."*

**Why this matters:**
- Goes 3-4 levels deeper on "why" (not just "it's a duty" but WHY it's a duty)
- Intellectual rigor: statement → why is that true? → why does THAT matter?
- Positive vision-focused (what you're building) vs. negative consequences
- Generational perspective: it's not just about you, it's about their future

---

### **3. Softened "Buying the Right to Mistreat" Language**

**Location:** Lines 122-128

**Before:**
> *"But what I didn't see: When I made respect the price of my good mood, what I was really saying was: 'I'm paying the bills, so I've earned the right to treat you however I want.'"*

**After:**
> *"But what I didn't see at the time—and honestly, if someone had pointed it out, I'd have denied it—is that when I made respect the price of my good mood, what I was really saying was: 'I'm paying the bills, so I've earned the right to treat you however I want.'*
>
> *I didn't see it that way then. But in hindsight, that was the effect of my actions. And there's no getting around it."*

**Why:**
- Acknowledges blindness at the time
- More empathetic tone (less likely to trigger defensiveness)
- Still makes the point clearly without pulling the punch
- Shows self-awareness without being performative
