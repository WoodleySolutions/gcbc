## 1. The Franchise System: Earned Citizenship

### Core Idea
Heinlein restricts voting rights to those who complete federal service. Only veterans can vote or hold office. This challenges modern assumptions about rights as unconditional entitlements.

### Catholic Contrast
- Rights flow from human dignity (imago Dei), not from merit or service
- Universal Declaration of Human Rights influenced by Jacques Maritain
- Franchise is both a right AND a responsibility
- The question isn't "who deserves to vote?" but "how do we form citizens in virtue?"

### Contemporary Application
- Declining voter participation
- Citizens as consumers of government services vs. active participants
- What obligations come with citizenship?
- Can democracy survive when rights are divorced from duties?

### Potential Content
- Blog post: "Should You Have to Earn the Right to Vote? What Starship Troopers Gets Wrong (and Right)"
- Video: "Catholic Social Teaching vs. Heinlein's Federation"
- Discussion prompt: Rights without responsibilities in contemporary America

## 2. Solidarity vs. Meritocracy

### Core Idea
Pope John Paul II's concept of solidarity: "a firm and persevering determination for the common good; that is to say, for the good of all and of each individual, because we are all really responsible for all."

### How It Relates to Starship Troopers
- Federation: prove your commitment through service, THEN you're part of the political community
- Solidarity: you're ALREADY responsible for all, because we're one human family
- Neither modern welfare state (rights without duties) nor Heinlein's meritocracy (duties to earn rights)

### Development Needed
- What does solidarity look like in practice?
- How does it differ from both individualism and collectivism?
- Application to contemporary politics

### Potential Content
- Essay: "Beyond Left and Right: What Catholic Solidarity Offers Politics"
- Comparison with other political philosophies
- Case studies of solidarity in action

## 3. Many Forms of Service and Sacrifice

### Core Idea
The Federation recognizes only military/federal service as politically significant. The Church recognizes many forms of sacrifice:
- Parents (especially mothers caring for children)
- Teachers accepting modest wages
- Caregivers for elderly/disabled
- Monks/nuns giving their lives to prayer
- Medical professionals serving the poor

### Catholic Teaching
All genuine service ordered toward the common good is valuable. A society that honors only one form (military) dishonors countless others.

### Contemporary Application
- "Thank you for your service" only for military
- Undervalued care work (mostly done by women)
- How do we honor non-military sacrifice?
- What forms of service go unrecognized in your community?

### Potential Content
- Blog post: "The Invisible Army: Honoring Service Beyond the Military"
- Video: "What Counts as Service?"
- Discussion question for meetings (this made it into the final 5)

## 4. Just War Theory and the Bug War

### Core Idea
The war against the Arachnids is presented as existential. But Heinlein never deeply explores whether it's a just war.

### Just War Criteria (Aquinas, Augustine)
- Just cause (self-defense)
- Legitimate authority
- Right intention
- Probability of success
- Proportionality
- Last resort
- Comparative justice

### Application to the Novel
- Was first contact handled properly?
- Are the bugs rational beings with whom negotiation is possible?
- Is total war (species extinction) ever justified?
- What does it mean to fight an enemy you can't communicate with?

### Contemporary Parallels
- War on Terror
- Preemptive strikes
- When is military force justified?
- How do we apply medieval just war theory to modern/future warfare?

### Potential Content
- Deep dive essay on just war theory using Starship Troopers as case study
- Video series on Catholic teaching about war
- Comparison with other books on the 2026 list (Master and Commander, etc.)

## 5. Authority Models: Zim, Frankel, Rasczak, Rico's Father

### Core Idea
The novel presents multiple models of authority:
- **Sergeant Zim**: Immediate superior, tough love, training-focused
- **Captain Frankel**: Career officer, mentor to Zim, wise and experienced
- **Lieutenant Rasczak**: Combat leader, beloved by his men, dies for them
- **Emilio Rico**: Paternal authority, civilian perspective, eventual respect for son's choice

### Analysis Needed
- What makes each effective or ineffective?
- How does each embody (or fail to embody) authority as service?
- Which most closely aligns with Catholic vision of authority?
- What can parents, managers, pastors learn from each?

### Potential Content
- Character study: "Four Models of Leadership in Starship Troopers"
- Application to different contexts (military, business, family, church)
- Discussion of Lieutenant Rasczak's death as sacrificial love

## 6. Rico's Transformation: Formation Through Hardship

### Core Idea
Juan Rico starts as spoiled rich kid, becomes hardened soldier, eventually officer. It's a bildungsroman about moral formation.

### Questions to Explore
- What specific virtues does he develop? (Courage, prudence, fortitude, justice)
- How does hardship shape character?
- What role does suffering play in formation?
- Could he have learned these lessons another way?

### Catholic Framework
- Suffering can be formative (not salvific, but formative)
- Discipline and rigor have a place in moral development
- But: formation serves the person, not the system
- The goal is virtue and holiness, not just competence

### Contemporary Application
- Helicopter parenting vs. formative hardship
- "Safe spaces" culture vs. resilience
- How do we prepare children for suffering without crushing them?
- Military schools, boot camps, monastic formation

### Potential Content
- Essay: "What Rico's Journey Teaches About Formation"
- Parent-focused content on formative hardship
- Comparison with other formation narratives in 2026 list

## 7. The Role of Women in the Federation

### Core Idea (Not Explored in Draft at All)
Women serve in the military (starship pilots) but the novel is almost entirely male-focused. Carmen Ibanez is Rico's love interest but also a capable officer.

### Questions

- How does military service affect traditional gender distinctions?
- What would Catholic teaching say about women in combat?
- Co-ed military vs. sex-segregated units

- Potentially contentious

## 9. Veterans in Contemporary Society

### Core Idea
The gap between military and civilian society in modern America. The all-volunteer force creates a warrior caste that most citizens never interact with.

### Questions
- What do we owe veterans?
- "Thank you for your service" as empty platitude vs. genuine honor
- Class disparities in military service (poor fight, rich don't)
- How do we integrate veterans back into civilian life?
- PTSD, suicide rates, homelessness among veterans

### Why It Was Cut
- Too contemporary/political
- Could easily become culture-war fodder
- Needs more space than available

### Potential Content
- Blog post during Veterans Day or Memorial Day
- Interview with Catholic veterans
- Discussion of how parishes can better serve military families

## 10. The Death Penalty and Military Justice

### Core Idea (Mentioned Briefly)
A soldier who went AWOL and murdered a baby is executed. Rico's unit carries out the execution. This is presented as just.

### Catholic Teaching
- Church now opposes death penalty in almost all cases (CCC 2267, updated 2018)
- Legitimate defense of society, but non-lethal means are sufficient
- Human dignity even for the worst criminals

### Questions
- Is military justice different from civilian justice?
- What about in wartime or extraterrestrial contexts?
- Does the Church's opposition to death penalty apply to military courts martial?
- What's the purpose of punishment? (Retribution, deterrence, rehabilitation, protection)

### Why It Was Cut
- One brief scene, not developed in the novel
- Would require extensive unpacking of Church teaching on capital punishment
- Potentially divisive topic

### Potential Content
- Separate essay on death penalty and Catholic teaching
- Use the scene as a jumping-off point for broader discussion
- Tie-in with other "justice" themed books in 2026
